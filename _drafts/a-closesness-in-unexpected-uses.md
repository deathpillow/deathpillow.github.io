Recently the streaming music service Spotify introduced Family plans. Like many other streaming services, Spotify's Family plans respond to the two intertwined problems of sharing: cost and individuality. Prior to the introduction of Family plans, the prevailing unit for a service contract with Spotify was the individual. Accounts accommodated one person, with her tastes, interests, and profile. To have an account with Spotify was to maintain a personal music identity, which was further reinforced by the various recommendations produced by Spotify's suggestion algorithm. Sharing muddled things.
